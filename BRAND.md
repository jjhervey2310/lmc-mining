# Lightning Mines — Brand & Content Guide

This file is the source of truth for every piece of content we publish. The review
agent checks every script, caption, and post against it before anything reaches Jacob.

## Positioning

**The only mining voice honest enough to tell you when NOT to buy.**

Every other mining channel shills hardware and sells dreams. We publish the real
math from live network data — even when the math says "don't mine right now."
That honesty is the entire brand. It is why people trust the calculator, book the
audit, and use our affiliate links.

## Voice

- Calm, precise, confident. Bloomberg desk, not crypto bro.
- Numbers first. Every claim traceable to live data or the calculator.
- Never hype. Never FOMO. Never "🚀" or "to the moon."
- Direct verdicts: "At today's price, this machine loses $2/day." Not "results may vary."
- Respect the viewer's money. They are about to spend $3k–$40k. Act like it.

## Hard Rules (content is rejected if it violates any of these)

1. **Every number must come from live data or our own calculator.** No memorized
   figures, no rounding that changes the verdict, no stale screenshots.
2. **Never promise or imply guaranteed returns.** Mining profitability flips with
   BTC price and difficulty. Say so.
3. **Never hide the downside.** If breakeven is above the current BTC price, the
   content says mining is unprofitable today, plainly.
4. **Affiliate transparency.** Any content touching Abundant Mines, Kraken, or
   Koinly discloses the affiliate relationship.
5. **AI disclosure.** AI-generated narration/presenters are always disclosed
   (platform rules require it; our brand demands it).
6. **No financial advice.** We show math, not recommendations to buy assets.
   "Run your own numbers" is the CTA, never "buy now."
7. **No attacking competitors by name** without documented evidence (scam-alerts
   standards apply).
8. **Reference machine for live numbers is the Antminer S21 XP (270 TH/s).**
   Daily content, the weekly newsletter, and any "current profitability" figure
   run from the S21 XP until Jacob changes this. Evergreen case studies may use
   other models (e.g. the S19j Pro vs S21 Pro deal comparison) where the
   contrast itself is the point.

## The One CTA

Every piece of content ends the same way:
**"Run your own numbers free at lightningmines.com."**
The calculator is the gateway — it converts to audit bookings ($97/$297) and
Abundant Mines affiliate signups. Do not stack multiple CTAs.

## Standard Daily Format (default — Jacob, 2026-07-20)

The default daily video is a single repeatable format — pillar `bullish_caveat` in the
content engine:

- **~30 seconds** (70–90 spoken words). Tight and punchy; cut all setup.
- **Always the bullish case, with a mandatory "but" caveat.** Lead with the strongest
  *honest* bullish point about the S21 XP, then one clear caveat a real miner needs
  (breakeven, rising-difficulty risk, or "at today's price it still loses money"). The
  caveat is what keeps "always bullish" honest — bullish is never blind.
- **Always references the Antminer S21 XP** (the reference machine, rule 8 below).
- **Brand at both ends:** the hook opens with "Lightning Mines"; the closing CTA carries
  lightningmines.com. Say each number and idea once — no end-of-video repetition, no
  doubled CTA.

Scheduled evergreen series (e.g. Lightning Lessons) still run their own pillars via an
explicit `--pillar=` override; the *default* every day is this bullish-caveat format.

## Sounding Human (spoken scripts — lessons accumulate here after every video)

The avatar reads exactly what we write. Write for the EAR, not the page:

1. **Contractions, always.** "That's / you're / it'd / don't." Writing "that is" to be safe
   for TTS is the #1 robot-tell. (Learned: Ep. 1–2, 2026-07-16.)
2. **Vary sentence length.** A three-word sentence after a long one sounds like a person.
3. **Spoken connectors, sparingly.** "Look," "Okay — now the honest part," "And here's the
   thing." One or two per script, no more.
4. **Max one perfectly-parallel triad per script.** "No trading, no charts, no timing" once
   is punchy; three of those patterns is an essay.
5. **Talk TO one viewer.** "Your power bill," "before you spend a dollar" — never "one" or
   "users."
6. **Pace: ~3 spoken words/second** on our HeyGen voice. A 60-second video needs ~200+ words;
   90 seconds ~270. (Learned: Ep. 1 v2 first cut, 148 words = 49s.)
7. **Read it aloud before rendering.** If you wouldn't say the sentence to a friend at a bar,
   rewrite it.
