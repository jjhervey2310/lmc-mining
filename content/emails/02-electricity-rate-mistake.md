The most expensive mistake in Bitcoin mining is also the most common: buying hardware before locking in a hosting rate.

Here is how it plays out. Someone finds an Antminer S21 Pro on sale. They buy it — $3,800 — because the hashprice looks good. That is the good outcome. The bad outcome is they sign anyway and BTC drops 20% two months in.

**The framework that prevents this:**

Work backwards. Before you evaluate any hardware, answer one question first: what electricity rate can I actually secure?

Contact two or three hosting providers. Get quotes in writing. Only after you have a confirmed rate should you look at hardware — because hardware is only valuable relative to the rate at which you can operate it.

The Antminer S21 Pro at $0.07/kWh generates approximately $21/day net at $100k BTC.
The same miner at $0.10/kWh generates approximately $8/day net.
The same miner at $0.12/kWh loses approximately $3/day.

Same hardware. Different rates. Three completely different businesses.

The hosting rate you lock in is the single variable you have the most control over before deployment. It is also the one most people spend the least time on.
