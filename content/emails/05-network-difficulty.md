Hi [First Name],

Every mining calculator shows you today's profitability. Most of them do not show you profitability 90 days from now.

That gap is where new miners get hurt.

**What network difficulty actually does:**

Bitcoin's protocol adjusts network difficulty every 2,016 blocks — approximately every two weeks. The adjustment targets 10 minutes per block by making the mining puzzle harder or easier depending on how much hashrate is competing for blocks.

In a bull market, higher BTC prices attract new miners. New miners deploy more hashrate. More hashrate means more competition. More competition means higher difficulty. Higher difficulty means your existing hardware earns less BTC per day.

From early 2024 to mid-2026, global network hashrate grew from approximately 550 EH/s to over 800 EH/s — a 45% increase. Every miner's earnings declined by 45% over that period, all else equal.

**The number you need to build in:**

In bull markets, model 20% annual difficulty growth as your baseline. Not because that is what will happen — it could be more or less — but because it is a reasonable conservative assumption that lets you see the floor of your returns.

At 20% annual difficulty growth, an S21 Pro earning $7.40/day net today earns approximately $6.17/day net in 12 months, assuming BTC price stays constant. Over 24 months: approximately $5.14/day.

That is still profitable. That is the point — good hardware and good hosting survive difficulty growth. But you need to model it honestly, not ignore it.

**How to model it:**

In the Mining ROI Spreadsheet I sent on day one, column G shows 12-month returns with a 20% difficulty growth assumption. Column H shows 24 months. Use those columns for any deal you are seriously evaluating.

If your deal only looks good in the static "today's difficulty" scenario, it is not a good deal.

**The self-correcting mechanism:**

When BTC price drops sharply, inefficient miners shut off because they cannot cover electricity costs. Hashrate falls. Difficulty decreases over subsequent adjustment periods. This benefits surviving miners — they earn more per unit of hashrate.

This is why efficient hardware gives you two advantages: more margin in bull markets, and the ability to outlast competitors in bear markets while they shut off and leave the network less crowded.

An S21 Pro at 15 J/TH stays profitable at difficulty levels that force older hardware — 25 J/TH and above — completely offline.
