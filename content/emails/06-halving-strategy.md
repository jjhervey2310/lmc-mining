Hi [First Name],

April 2028 is the next Bitcoin halving. Block rewards drop from 3.125 BTC to 1.5625 BTC. Every miner's BTC earnings are cut in half on that day.

For most operators, this is the most important planning constraint in their business model. Here is how to think about it.

**The direct math:**

An S21 Pro today earns approximately $14.90/day gross at $100k BTC.
After the 2028 halving, at the same BTC price and difficulty: approximately $7.45/day gross.
At $225/month hosting ($7.50/day): breakeven — essentially zero net margin.

That means at $100k BTC, S21 Pro operators are at breakeven immediately post-halving. For the deal to remain profitable, BTC must rise above $100k post-halving — or difficulty must fall as less-efficient miners exit, which historically happens as miners get squeezed out.

**What history says:**

The three previous halvings (2016, 2020, 2024) each produced significant BTC price appreciation within 12–18 months of the halving event. This is not guaranteed — past halvings do not ensure future price performance — but the supply-shock mechanism is well-understood.

In each prior cycle, post-halving difficulty initially fell as marginal miners exited, then rose sharply as higher prices attracted new investment. The surviving miners — those with efficient hardware and competitive hosting — compounded returns through the down period and the subsequent bull market.

**The strategic implication:**

Hardware deployed today should either:

1. Pay for itself entirely before April 2028 — which is achievable for S21 Pro at current prices, where the 18-month payback timeline lands well before the halving, or

2. Be efficient enough to remain profitable on post-halving economics — which the S21 Pro is at BTC prices above approximately $100k.

Hardware above 20 J/TH will be under significant pressure post-halving unless BTC appreciates substantially from current levels. This is the argument for not buying older-generation hardware at any price today — the 2028 halving is a hard ceiling on its economic viability.

**One more thing:**

The halving is also the reason I emphasize flat-fee hosting over per-kWh for most operators. When difficulty falls post-halving (as marginal miners exit), your per-kWh electricity bill stays fixed while your BTC earnings per unit of hashrate actually improve. Flat-fee hosting lets you capture that improvement fully.
