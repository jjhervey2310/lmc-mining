Hi [First Name],

Most hosting contracts are written by the hosting company's lawyers, for the hosting company's benefit. That is not a criticism — it is just how contracts work. Your job is to ask the right questions before you sign, because almost nothing in a hosting contract will tell you whether the deal is actually good.

Here are the 12 questions I run through every time.

**The 12 questions:**

1. Is pricing flat-fee per machine or per kWh? Flat-fee is almost always preferable — your cost is predictable regardless of your miner's actual power draw.

2. What is the all-in monthly cost per machine, including cooling, maintenance, and internet?

3. What uptime guarantee do you offer? What is the SLA if you miss it — credit, refund, or nothing?

4. What is the minimum contract term? Is there an early exit provision, and what does it cost?

5. How much is the security deposit, and under what conditions is it returned?

6. What is your power source? Hydroelectric is the most cost-stable. Grid power creates rate risk.

7. What cooling type — air, immersion, or hydro? Your hardware must be compatible. Most standard hosting is air-cooled.

8. Can you provide dated photos or video of the facility, including my machines after onboarding?

9. Who is my point of contact if a machine goes offline at 2am? Is there an on-call team?

10. Can I visit the facility before signing?

11. Do I retain legal ownership of my hardware throughout the contract? What happens to it if the company fails?

12. Have you ever had downtime longer than 48 hours? What caused it and how was it resolved?

**Red flags that end the conversation:**

Any provider who cannot answer questions 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, and 11 clearly and in writing is not a provider you should do business with. These are not unreasonable questions — they are minimum due diligence.

Additional red flags: large non-refundable deposits (over $1,000), no named emergency contact, auto-renewing contracts with no notice requirement, and refusal to allow a facility visit.
