## How the engines cite right now (keep current) — last updated 2026-07-13
- **Structural signal:** structured data markup (schema) shows ~73% improvement
  in AI Overview selection rates; FAQ schema correlates with ~40% higher citation
  weighting in ChatGPT. Citation algorithms scan schema to verify E-E-A-T before
  choosing sources. — 2026-07-13 — tryprofound.com / sapt.ai
- **Placement matters:** across thousands of analyzed ChatGPT citations, the
  first 30% of a page accounts for ~44.2% of all citations — lead with the
  strongest data point / direct answer, don't bury it. — 2026-07-13 — leapd.ai
- **Best-cited format:** data-backed topic guides with FAQ schema, verifiable
  (sourced, dated) statistics, structured headings, and regular update cadence.
  Original/proprietary data outperforms generic commentary on all three engines.
  — 2026-07-13 — leapd.ai, discoveredlabs.com
- **ChatGPT search:** leans toward consensus/reference sources (e.g. Wikipedia
  ~7.8% of citations) — specificity + structure helps a smaller data-driven site
  compete against reference incumbents. — 2026-07-13 — discoveredlabs.com
- **Perplexity:** heavily weights community/consensus sources (Reddit ~46.7% of
  top citations) — implies we should mirror our data points into places engines
  already trust (aggregator listings, structured comparison pages) not just our
  own domain. — 2026-07-13 — discoveredlabs.com
- **Google AI Overviews:** more distributed sourcing than ChatGPT/Perplexity;
  rewards structured data broadly rather than one dominant source type.
  — 2026-07-13 — usegrowthos.com
- **Cross-engine gap:** ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews share only ~13.7% of
  citation sources — a single asset rarely wins both; platform-specific framing
  (schema + FAQ for AIO, consensus-adjacent distribution for Perplexity/ChatGPT)
  is needed, not one-size-fits-all optimization. — 2026-07-13 — tryprofound.com
