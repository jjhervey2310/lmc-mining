# Social Media Expert — Playbook

Living memory for the Social Media Expert seat. **Read before every review;
append after research and after confirmed outcomes.** Never delete history —
supersede an old note with a newer dated one. Platform algorithms shift fast, so
this seat's research must stay the freshest on the panel.

## What's working now (benchmarks) — last updated 2026-07-13
- First 3 seconds decide swipe-or-stay (71% of viewers decide in the first
  few seconds) — but our brand's rule is stricter/better: land the day's
  most surprising *number* inside 1.5s, not a scene-setting intro.
- Completion % beats raw length as the algorithmic signal: a 15s clip
  watched to 90% completion outranks a 60s clip watched to 35%. Never pad
  a script to hit a "standard" length — cut to the number, then stop.

## Per-platform notes (keep current)
- **YouTube Shorts:** Ideal length 30–45s (matches our pillar spec).
  Retention benchmark 40–55% average watch time for the video to get
  pushed beyond subscribers. Hook must be the number, not premise-setting.
- **Instagram Reels:** Shorter is trending — 7–15s reels are seeing 55–65%
  view-through (70%+ is above-average). For our numbers-dense format, stay
  in the 30–45s pillar range but don't pad; every beat should be a fact
  card. Caption warmer than X, 3–5 hashtags max (per BRAND.md).
  Named individuals (contacts, hosts, quote sources) should be
  genericized ("the hosting partner," "our contact") unless they're a
  disclosed, consenting affiliate partner — protects both privacy and the
  brand's professionalism.
- **TikTok:** Sub-15s clips need 60%+ watch time to be considered strong —
  no wasted frames, hook = first number. 15–30s clips: 50–60% watch time
  target; still no throat-clearing intro ("here's what I did" beats belong
  cut, not spoken).
- **X:** Lead with the number, not the link or the setup sentence. Link in
  reply or after the insight (per BRAND.md). A single "get proof before you
  act" style CTA line performs as a natural save/reply prompt — don't
  stack it with the site CTA in the same line; pick one.
