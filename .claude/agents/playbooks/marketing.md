## What's working now (benchmarks) — last updated 2026-07-13
- "Build in public" / behind-the-decision content (real capital-deployment
  calls, named specifics, admitted trade-offs) outperforms generic "ultimate
  guide" content 3-5x on engagement, and named-contributor pieces with a
  concrete, sometimes-controversial opinion beat anonymous brand-voice posts
  on both organic engagement and AI-citation frequency. This is a strong
  native fit for our "tell you when NOT to buy" positioning — but raw internal
  notes still need audience framing (who/what is this, defined terms) and the
  one CTA before they qualify as publishable brand content. Source: search
  results synthesized 2026-07-13 (schemaninja.com, ascendlymarketing.com,
  paceco.com — see Research log).

## Research log (append newest on top)
- 2026-07-13 — "Build in public" transparency content (frank capital-deployment
  decisions, post-mortems, named specificity) drives 3-5x higher engagement
  than generic guide content; audiences reward vulnerability + verifiable
  specificity over polish; named contributors with a concrete opinion
  outperform anonymous brand copy. Why it matters: our internal "here's how
  I'm actually deploying capital" memos are a high-potential content format
  if reshaped for a cold audience (context, defined terms, single CTA) rather
  than posted as raw notes. — sources: https://schemaninja.com/news/transparent-content-marketing-2026/, https://ascendlymarketing.com/transparent-marketing-build-trust-and-drive-real-growth/, https://www.paceco.com/insights/content-marketing-trust/
