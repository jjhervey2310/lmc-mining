## Recurring temptations (patterns that keep trying to slip through)
<!-- the off-brand move — why it's tempting — the guard that stops it -->
- **Asymmetric hedging (the "good news doesn't need the caveat" move)** — a piece
  correctly hedges the *unprofitable* scenario ("at current BTC price and difficulty")
  but drops that same hedge when stating the *profitable* scenario ("it's a real 3-4
  year payback with fleet upside"), so the bad news reads as conditional/honest and the
  good news reads as settled fact. Tempting because it feels balanced — one side has a
  caveat, so the piece "looks" hedged overall — but the omission on the upside is exactly
  where Hard Rule 2 (no guaranteed/implied returns) bites, since payback-period claims are
  the most fragile to price/difficulty drift over a multi-year horizon. Guard: every
  forward-looking number (payback period, ROI, "upside") needs the *same* price/difficulty
  caveat as any downside number in the same piece, or it's a caps-the-score violation.

- 2026-07-13 — FTC guaranteed-earnings enforcement remains a live priority (2024-2025 actions
  cite deceptive "guaranteed income" claims; 2025 ANPRM proposes expanding Business
  Opportunity Rule to money-making/investment opportunities, requiring written substantiation
  for any earnings claim). Confirms our Hard Rule 2 standard is not just brand preference —
  unhedged multi-year payback/ROI projections are exactly the claim type regulators target.

  technical presence — disclosure must sit next to the claim, not buried — relevant if any

  (Jan 2025), ftc.gov Endorsement Guides FAQ, thesocialmedialawfirm.com 2026 guide.

## Lessons learned (mistakes → rule) (append newest on top)
<!-- YYYY-MM-DD — what slipped or was over-flagged — the rule that calibrates it -->
- 2026-07-13 — LMC Phase 1 Deployment Plan v2 (internal memo) rewrote the whole body
  with real hedging (BTC-price reality check, downside stated plainly, hardware cost
  flagged as an estimate/range) but the verbatim "Bottom line" — the one sentence a
  reader actually acts on before wiring money — kept "Under $0.06/kWh, it's a real
  3-4 year payback with fleet upside" almost unchanged from the v1 draft that got
  vetoed for this exact clause, and dropped the price/difficulty caveat that the same
  paragraph's downside sentence didn't need to drop (its downside sentence stayed
  self-contained: "don't pay back inside a reasonable investment horizon"). Calibrated
  rule: don't grade a document's hedging by its aggregate density — locate the literal
  final "bottom line" / TL;DR sentence(s) a reader will act on and check *that specific
  sentence* against Hard Rule 2 in isolation, independent of how well-hedged the body
  is. A well-hedged body does not retroactively hedge an unhedged punchline, and
  unquantified bonus claims tacked onto a conditional ("with fleet upside") are a
  variant of the same asymmetric-hedging move, not a separate issue.
