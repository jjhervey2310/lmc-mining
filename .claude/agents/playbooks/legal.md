## Current rules reference (keep current) — last updated 2026-07-13
- Affiliate disclosure (FTC): Endorsement Guides (16 CFR 255) require any material
  connection (commission, referral fee, free product) be disclosed clear &
  conspicuously — near the link (proximity), as prominent as surrounding text
  (prominence), and seen before/at the same time as the link (unavoidability).
  2026 enforcement guidance extends the obligation through the full affiliate
  chain (e.g., a named hosting contact who routes to a paid partner still needs
  disclosure). Penalties up to ~$51,744/violation. — 2026-07-13 — ftc.gov
  Endorsement Guides FAQ; 16 CFR 255.
- AI-content disclosure (per platform): unchanged from BRAND.md — disclose AI
  narration/presenters in description/caption per platform rules.
- Financial-promotion / no-advice line: SEC/CFTC joint interpretive guidance
  (Securities Act Release No. 33-11412, issued 2026-03-17) reframes the Howey
  "investment contract" analysis around *representations or promises of profit
  tied to a third party's essential managerial efforts*, conveyed prior to or
  contemporaneous with an offer/sale. Relevance to us: content that pairs a
  payback/return claim with a specific third-party-managed arrangement (e.g., a
  named hosting operator handling the mining) is closer to an "investment
  contract" fact pattern than generic educational math is. Mitigant: keep
  claims framed as historical/current-data math the reader must re-run
  ("run your own numbers"), avoid declarative future-tense promises ("it's a
  real payback"), and always restate that BTC price/difficulty govern the
  outcome. — 2026-03-17 guidance date / logged 2026-07-13 — sec.gov 33-11412;
  cftc.gov joint release.

## Research log (append newest on top)
- 2026-07-13 — Logged FTC Endorsement Guide "clear/conspicuous" 3-part test
  (proximity, prominence, unavoidability) and SEC/CFTC 2026-03-17 interpretive
  release on investment-contract analysis (profit representations tied to a
  third party's managerial efforts). Effect: named third-party hosting/return
  claims now get flagged for (a) affiliate-chain disclosure even if the link
  itself isn't ours, and (b) investment-contract-adjacent framing when a
  payback claim is declarative rather than hedged/re-runnable by the reader.
  Sources: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftcs-endorsement-guides-what-people-are-asking ;
  https://www.sec.gov/files/rules/interp/2026/33-11412.pdf

## Lessons learned (mistakes → rule) (append newest on top)
- 2026-07-13 — v2 of a previously-VETOed "deployment plan" reframed as an
  **internal** capital-deployment memo (Jacob/LMC's own decision, not
  published, not an offer to any third party). Calibrated rule: FTC
  Endorsement Guides (16 CFR 255) attach to disclosures made *to an audience*
  (consumers/viewers of an endorsement); SEC/CFTC 33-11412 investment-contract
  analysis attaches to representations made *in connection with an offer/sale
  to a third-party investor*. Neither element (audience, offer/sale) is
  present in a solo/self-directed internal memo — so the affiliate-disclosure
  and investment-contract-adjacency flags from the v1 VETO do not carry over
  automatically to v2's internal-use case. What DOES carry over unconditionally:
  a declarative, unhedged forward-looking payback line ("it's a real 3-4 year
  payback") is still the thing to fix the moment the document's audience
  changes — i.e., before it is published, pitched to a co-investor/lender, or
  otherwise leaves Jacob's own hands. Rule going forward: score internal-only
  memos on their actual (internal) legal surface, but always append an
  explicit "gate" condition — the specific line(s) that must be hedged/
  disclosed before the same document could be shown to anyone else — so the
  conditional risk isn't lost between drafts.
- 2026-07-13 — "Deployment plan" style content that verdicts a *specific named
  hosting deal* ("this tranche is good if Immanuel quotes under $0.06/kWh")
  reads as personalized investment counsel, not generic educational math, even
  though it shows real math and states the downside. Calibrated rule: any
  asset that (1) names a specific third-party operator arranging the hosting/
  mining, (2) states a payback/return conclusion in declarative present tense
  ("it's a real 3-4 year payback") rather than conditional/hedged terms, and
  (3) directs a specific capital action ("before wiring anything") needs (a) a
  restated price/difficulty-volatility hedge next to the forward payback claim,
  (b) an explicit not-financial-advice line, and (c) confirmation/disclosure of
  whether the named operator is a paid affiliate/referral before it can
  publish as brand content. Two of three present here → capped at VETO
  pending fixes, not just "tighten before ship."
